Exercise Demonstration

Raise

The exercise shown in the image is commonly known as a leg raise, which is a core-strengthening exercise that primarily targets the abdominal muscles. How to perform a leg raise: Lie on your back on a mat with your arms at your sides, palms facing down. Keep your legs straight and together. Engage your core muscles and lift your legs off the ground, keeping them straight, until they are perpendicular to the floor. Slowly lower your legs back down to the starting position, controlling the movement. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Crunch

The exercise shown in the image is a crunch, specifically an abdominal crunch or sit-up. It is a common exercise targeting the abdominal muscles. Here is how it's performed: Starting Position: Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Hands can be placed behind the head or across the chest. Movement: Engage your abdominal muscles to lift your head and shoulder blades off the floor. The lower back should remain in contact with the floor. Return: Slowly lower back down to the starting position.

Abdominal Remado

The exercise shown in the image is called the "Abdominal Remador". It is a core exercise that works the abdominal muscles. To perform the exercise, one starts in a seated position with knees bent and feet slightly off the ground, then lowers the upper body while extending the legs, and returns to the starting position. The movement resembles rowing, hence the name "Remador," which means "rower" in Portuguese.

Bicycle Crunch

The exercise shown in the image is commonly known as a "bicycle crunch" or "bicycle sit-up". It is a variation of the traditional crunch that targets the obliques and abdominal muscles. How to perform a bicycle crunch: Lie on the back with knees bent and feet lifted slightly off the floor. Place hands behind the head, lightly supporting it, without pulling on the neck. Engage the core and bring the right elbow towards the left knee while extending the right leg. Alternate sides, bringing the left elbow towards the right knee and extending the left leg. Continue alternating sides in a pedaling motion. Bicycle crunches are effective for strengthening core muscles, improving stability, and enhancing overall fitness. They are a popular exercise choice for individuals seeking to target their abs and obliques.

Push-Ups

The exercise shown in the image is called a push-up. It is a bodyweight exercise that primarily works the muscles of the chest, shoulders, and triceps. Push-ups also engage the core muscles and can help to improve posture and balance. To perform a standard push-up: Begin in a plank position with hands shoulder-width apart, palms flat on the floor, and body in a straight line from head to heels. Lower the body towards the floor, keeping the elbows at a 45-degree angle. Push back up to the starting position.

Runch and Punch

he exercise shown in the image is commonly known as the "Crunch and Punch". This exercise targets the abdominal muscles and obliques, while also engaging the arms and shoulders. Here's how it is performed: Start by sitting on the floor with knees bent and feet slightly elevated. Lean back slightly, engaging the core muscles. Perform a crunch, lifting the upper body towards the knees. Simultaneously, twist the torso and extend one arm forward in a punching motion. Alternate the punching arm with each crunch, engaging the obliques. Repeat the movement for the desired number of repetitions.

Dumbbell Lunge

The exercise shown in the image is a dumbbell lunge, specifically a reverse lunge with dumbbells. Here's a breakdown of the exercise: Starting Position: The person is standing with feet hip-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand. Movement: They step backward with one leg, lowering the body until both knees are bent at approximately 90-degree angles. The front knee is aligned over the ankle, and the back knee hovers just above the ground. The torso is upright, and the core is engaged. They push off with the front foot to return to the starting position.

Lunge

The exercise shown in the image is called a lunge. It is a lower-body exercise that works several muscle groups, including the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and calves. Lunges are a versatile exercise that can be modified to increase or decrease the difficulty, and they can be done with or without weights. Here's how to properly perform a lunge: Start by standing with feet hip-width apart. Step forward with one leg, lowering your body until both knees are bent at a 90-degree angle. Make sure the front knee is directly over the ankle and the back knee is not touching the ground. Push off with the front foot to return to the starting position. Repeat on the other side.

Side Plank with Twist

The exercise shown in the image is called a Side Plank with Twist. It is a variation of the standard side plank that adds a rotational movement to engage the core muscles further and improve spinal mobility. To perform this exercise, one should: Start in a side plank position, either on the knees or feet, with the body in a straight line. Place the top hand behind the head or on the hip. Rotate the torso forward, bringing the top elbow towards the ground while maintaining stability in the hips and core. Return to the starting position and repeat for the desired number of repetitions before switching sides.
